A few weeks ago, I received an invitation from **Boubacar Camara**, Ambassador at 
Université Alioune Diop for the GalsenAI program, to speak at a community meetup 
on Machine Learning. I said yes,  adding another chapter to a speaking journey 
I've been building for a while now.

## The Topic

The session was titled **"Understanding Machine Learning and Building Your First ML 
Model"**, covering classification and regression for an audience of around 17 participants.

## What I Covered

The session was structured around 5 key moments:

**1. What is Machine Learning?**
Starting with a clear, accessible definition, no jargon, just the core idea of 
machines learning from data.

**2. How does a model actually learn?**
Walking through the intuition behind training,  features, labels, loss, and iteration.

**3. Classification vs Regression**
Explaining the two fundamental supervised learning paradigms with real-world examples 
everyone in the room could relate to.

**4. A Real ML Workflow**
A hands-on walkthrough of a small end-to-end pipeline,  from raw data to a working model.

**5. Q&A,  Open Floor**
Some of the best questions came here. The audience was curious, engaged, and hungry to learn.

## What I Took Away

Speaking forces you to truly understand what you know — and honestly reveal what you 
don't. Preparing this session pushed me to go deeper on concepts I thought I already 
understood.

Seeing 17 people genuinely engaged with Machine Learning for the first time reminded 
me why sharing knowledge matters. This is exactly why I started this blog.

## What's Next

This was my first talk featured on this blog — but certainly not my first time 
on a stage. More sessions, more topics, more communities to come.

If you're organizing a community event around AI, Cloud, or Machine Learning and 
need a speaker — feel free to reach out.
